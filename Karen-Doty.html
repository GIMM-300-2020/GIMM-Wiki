Karen Doty

Career at GIMM

 Karen Doty is  passionate about evolving learning opportunities provided by emerging technologies. By working as an educator for GIMM, she is able to guide students toward a career path that is essential to future societal endeavors. Since arriving at Boise State in 2017, Doty has taught animation, game development, user experience and senior capstone courses for the GIMM department. During graduate school, her research expanded to working with  gaming interfaces, virtual reality and mixed reality experiences.

 

Background

Dr. Doty grew up in Iowa where she worked as a graphic designer and journalist after undergraduate college. She has always been interested in emerging technology, however her focus on human computer interaction and specific user experience studies began during extensive research at graduate school.

 

Industry Experience

Karen Doty attended Truman State University in Kirksville, Missouri; where she received both a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Visual Communication and a Bachelor of Arts in Journalism. After undergrad, she worked as a graphic designer, illustrator and muralist in Des Moines, Iowa. She returned to graduate school at Iowa State University where she received an Masters of Fine Arts in graphic design and  a Ph.D. in human computer interaction. Her research focused on user experience practices in evolving technologies including eBooks, wearable mixed reality and virtual user interfaces. Previously she taught as an instructor at Grand View University teaching interactive media and mobile app development. 

Additionally, she acts as faculty advisor for the Boise State NASA-SUITS program, a student-led group focusing on development and design for mixed reality applications using the Microsoft HoloLens.

 

Interesting Facts

She continues to paint large scale murals and canvas artwork using mixed media with ink, acrylic paint and conte chalk.
